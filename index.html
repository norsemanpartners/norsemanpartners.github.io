About Us

Stanford students passionate about disrupting the mining industry.

Founded out of the Stanford GSB class ‘Building a Money Management Business’ by a group of highly-driven students, Norseman Partners is an emerging mining-focused venture capital firm – the first of its kind in the mining industry.

Founding partners Luke Ren and Declan Schuller hail from the Australian Outback. They have a proven identified promising post-discovery mining opportunities, and, with a team of the best technical experts in the industry, drove them through the early stages of commercialization and proving-out to the wider market to achieve strong venture style outcomes for their investors.

With that experience driving a conviction that it is possible to reliably identify and progress top decile minerals opportunities ahead of the market, Luke and Declan entered Stanford GSB together to learn how to institutionalize the concept to capture more opportunities, deliver stronger returns, and run better businesses while keeping responsibility to investors at the core.

With the world rapidly changing, investing in early-stage mining is a compelling proposition: a wide-open opportunity field with rapidly changing industries, growth in new minerals powering new and vital industries, a lack of institutional competition, and exposure to high returning economics with the underlying security of investing in hard-assets underwritten by secular growth.

With an experienced and existing team of mining investors and developers, Norseman Partners doesn’t just pick winners – it makes them.

Richmond Vanadium

Sourced in December 2016 following ongoing review of commodities markets identifying vanadium as an exponential growth opportunity Identified ‘Lilyvale’ as the third largest vanadium deposit in the world with high grade potential through deep industry knowledge and connections Leveraged existing relationships with major shareholders of owner (Horizon Minerals) to gain access and completed negotiations by March 2017 ‘Earn-in’ agreement (no upfront cost) delivering management rights and 75% ownership by spending A$6M by March 2021 Vanadium price rose from $5/lb at acquisition to $28/lb by December 2018 Deployed in-house team of four mining professionals who part-time managed contractors and consultants to prove out project economics and complete initial plans for development (pre-feasibility phase) Pre-feasibility study completed by August 2021 identifying project NPV10 of A$1.1B and IRR of 62% with highest quartile production quality and lowest quartile operating costs Successful IPO in November 2022 at A$53.8M pre-money valuation

Doublebell Resource Wealth

Sourced in January 2018 following on-ground international experience identifying kaolin production as a secretive, largely private industry with growing demand from diversified industries but approaching supply exhaustion in key producers (China, US, UK) Identified Bromus deposit as the largest known single-orebody reserve of kaolin based on previous experience and industry knowledge in the area - previous explorers found high-grade kaolin but were searching for nickel Leveraged credibility and reputation of in-house team to approach owners of the fragmented ‘Bromus’ deposit and bought them out to consolidate reserve Spent A$6M on upfront acquisitions costs Single part-time manager who managed further A$500K in expenditure to complete pre-feasibility phase by March 2022 Term sheet from private equity for $7.5M in further investment (sufficient to get to revenue) at a $25M pre-money valuation with partial exit option

Echo Resources

Failed investment in public company Sourced in August 2015 through industry monitoring and leveraging knowledge and connections to drive deeper insights Major high grade gold discovery in listed exploration company but with slow progression Insight: exploration focused management delaying development – classic ‘mining the market’ instead of mining the resource due to a lack of capacity to do anything else Leveraged existing connections with major shareholder to gain access Entered in August 2015 through off-market purchase of 16% of the company for A$3.4M ($0.15/share, ~23M shares) Led changes to chairman and managing director in February 2016 Was forced to liquidate at a loss due to cornerstone investor needing liquidity to service debt – off-market sale in March 2016 at A$3M ($400K loss) Company bought out (98% stake) by strategic acquirer in October 2019 at A$0.33/share This is why we are seeking to institutionalize

Gold Ridge Mining

Sourced in September 2016 following industry monitoring and deep connections which surfaced the real story Large high grade gold deposit in Solomon Islands owned by listed company with continual performance problems and public explanations which did not add up Understood real story was incompetent technical and ESG management driving low productivity and poor relations with local community Project abandoned by previous owners in April 2014 Flew into country in January 2015 and built connections with local community and leaders and supported them to take over project Local community took over project for liability (largely environmental damage to local community) in May 2015 In September 2016 formalized agreement to enter into 90:10 JV with the local community in exchange for funding and progressing project into redevelopment Hired two in-country expatriate managers (financing and community relations), and led technical studies remotely through two members of existing team in Australia (geologist, engineer) who worked on project part time Spent A$8M over three years on environmental rehabilitation, and developing recommissioning plans and cost estimates By July 2017 had strategic acquirer lined up Partially exited in July 2019 to strategic acquirer who purchased 55% in exchange for A$58M and agreed to commit a further A$50M towards redevelopment
